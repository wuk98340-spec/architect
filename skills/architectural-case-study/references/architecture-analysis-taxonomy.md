# Architecture Analysis Taxonomy

Use this reference to select professional analysis dimensions for an architecture case. Do not mechanically apply every dimension. Identify the project's strongest 3-5 strategies, then expand only those that are supported by sources, drawings, images, or clear project evidence.

## Strategy Dimensions

- Site strategy: terrain, block structure, urban edge, landscape view, historical context, access sequence.
- Program strategy: mixed use, zoning, vertical organization, circulation, public/private gradient, pedestrian/vehicle separation.
- Spatial strategy: sequence, publicness, openness, threshold, courtyard, atrium, corridor, roof, shared space.
- Massing strategy: volume generation, setback, cutting, lifting, courtyard formation, tower-podium relation.
- Facade strategy: envelope, opening, shading, curtain wall, proportion, solid/void rhythm, texture.
- Structural strategy: long span, cantilever, prefabrication, timber, steel, concrete, hybrid system.
- Material and tectonic strategy: material choice, local material, new material, detail expression, construction logic.
- Regional culture translation: traditional space, local symbols, craft, climate, contemporary life pattern.
- Green and climate strategy: natural ventilation, daylighting, shading, rainwater, vertical greenery, low-carbon material.
- User experience: route, pause, viewing, social activity, learning, consumption, ritual, arrival.
- Transferable lesson: what architecture students, competitors, case-library editors, or design researchers can learn.

## Case Type Emphasis

- Cultural building: emphasize publicness, urban interface, exhibition route, symbolic form, civic identity.
- Education building: emphasize learning space, informal exchange, campus relationship, flexible use.
- Hotel: emphasize arrival, view, guestroom module, public zone, facade landmark quality.
- Housing / apartment: emphasize unit type, view, privacy, amenities, facade order.
- Commercial complex: emphasize circulation, tenant mix, interface, entries, vertical transport, consumption route.
- Renovation: emphasize old-new relation, retained structure, material dialogue, historical memory.
- Rural / cultural tourism: emphasize local material, village fabric, operation model, low-cost construction.
- Green building: emphasize climate response, energy strategy, construction details, ecological systems.

## Strategy Writing Requirements

For each selected strategy, write:

- Strategy name.
- Design problem: the constraint, need, or opportunity the strategy responds to.
- Specific approach: what the project does spatially, tectonically, formally, or operationally.
- Architectural effect: what the strategy changes in use, experience, performance, or urban relation.
- Evidence source: source IDs, source links, drawings, image IDs, or clear notes that the claim is AI synthesis.
- Related image or drawing link: include when a plan, section, facade, diagram, or photo directly supports the claim.
- Transferable lesson: what can be learned without copying the project literally.

## Evidence Discipline

- Prefer drawings, diagrams, official descriptions, interviews, and high-quality architecture media for strategy claims.
- Mark inferred ideas as AI synthesis when the source does not directly state them.
- Do not fabricate structure, material, climate performance, area, or year.
- If evidence is weak, reduce certainty and move the issue to `uncertain_or_conflicting_info`.
